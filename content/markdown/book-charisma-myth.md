### Charisma as Skill

- A powerful indicator of business success.
- Heavily linked to body language.
- Not "always on."
- Charismatic people are seen as:
  - Powerful
  - Strong in presence
  - Warm and interested in people
- Warmth comes from goodwill and caring.
- Presence matters more than attractiveness.
- Charisma is increased by exercise.
- What your mind believes, your body manifests.

### Four Types of Charisma

- **Focus Charisma (Presence):** Communicates respect for others' opinions.
- **Visionary Charisma (Belief):** Inspires with conviction; radiates acceptance rather than criticism.
- **Authority Charisma (Status):** Signals power and importance; discourages feedback.
- **Kindness Charisma (Warmth):** Signals acceptance and promotes emotional bonding.

##### Additional Notes

- People remember first and last impressions most strongly.
- Envy prevention framework: **JAIR**
  - Justify
  - Appreciate others' actions
  - Define their Impact
  - Give others Responsibility

#### Charisma in Communication

- Use lower intonation at the end of sentences.
- Reduce nodding.
- Use pauses.
- Avoid distraction (it is highly visible in body language).
- Increase concentration on people speaking.
- Mental state is reflected by the body (anxiety, dissatisfaction, self-doubt, internal negativity).
- Facial expressions are universal.
- First impressions can define the whole relationship.
- A strong handshake creates a major first impression.

#### Good Handshake

- Stand straight.
- Keep hands out of pockets.
- Maintain strong eye contact.
- Keep head straight.
- Shake from the elbow.
- Extend warmth.

#### During Conversation

- When leaving a conversation, offer value:
  - Information
  - Connection
  - Recognition
- People remember feelings more than words.

#### Good Listening

- Never interrupt.
- Pause before replying.
- Let facial expression respond first, then use words.

#### Charismatic Speaking

- Highlight the good.
- Fully absorb and appreciate compliments.
- Do not try to impress; let others impress you.
- Use graphic explanations.
- Trigger good emotions after listening (entertainment, information, laughter).

#### Vocal and Physical Presence

- Lower baritone tones are more impactful.
- Modulate volume to empower key words.
- Slow tempo and use frequent pauses.
- Smiling adds warmth to words, even on phone calls.
- Sitting next to someone reduces confrontation compared with face-to-face.
- Intense eye contact can have powerful impact.
- Posture communicates internal emotion.

#### Confident Body Language Routine

- Breathe calmly.
- Stand up.
- Shake out your body.
- Take a wide stance.
- Stretch arms to the ceiling.
- Stretch arms to the side.
- Bring arms back down.
- Roll shoulders.
- Inflate posture (take up space).

#### Additional Communication Guidance

- Fidgeting, excess nodding, and too much movement communicate low status.
- Avoid anger.
- Structure bad news:
  - Depersonalize (behavior > person)
  - Be specific
  - Empathize
- Reduce defensiveness by bringing up past positives and signaling goodwill.
- For apologies:
  - Forgive yourself first
  - Listen fully
  - Do not interrupt
  - Prefer "I'm sorry"
  - Show understanding of impact
- Never multitask on phone calls.
- Counteract shame in others with empathy, sharing, and appreciation.
- In the spotlight:
  - Accept attention
  - Show vulnerability
  - Show emotion
  - Mention nervousness if present

### Charisma Mentality

- Dissatisfaction is often caused by comparison.
- Self-criticism triggers internal threat response and reduces brain power.
- Gratitude helps contentment, which supports charisma.
- General goodwill is visible through body language.
- Compassion = goodwill + empathy.
- Progression: **Empathy -> Sympathy -> Compassion**
- Self-confidence: belief in ability.
- Self-esteem: how much we value ourselves as a person.
- Self-compassion: how much warmth we have for ourselves.
- Focusing on self-compassion is healthier than focusing on self-esteem; it helps recovery after mistakes and inaction.
- When down, self-compassion helps emotional healing.
- Body language can change mood:
  - Smiling
  - Energetic movement
  - Powerful stance
- Logic makes you think; emotion makes you act.
- Emotions are contagious.
- Mirroring body language can build connection.

#### Charisma Practices

##### 1) Responsibility Transfer

- Close eyes and get comfortable.
- Take 3 deep breaths; exhale strongly, imagining worries leaving with the breath.
- Imagine spiritual consciousness carrying away burdens.
- Imagine troubles lifted and release the need to control outcomes.

##### 2) De-stigmatize Discomfort

- Reiterate that discomfort is normal and everyday.
- Accept there is no single "right way" to feel.
- Examine feelings as information.
- Remember emotions are an evolutionary by-product.
- Reject shame (the most destructive response).
- Remind yourself the burden is widely shared.

##### 3) Metta - Self-Compassion Exercise

- Close eyes and get comfortable.
- Focus on a memory of a good, courageous, or truthful moment.
- Think of someone who has strong affection for you.
- Picture them radiating warmth, kindness, and compassion.
- See yourself through their eyes, and feel forgiveness and acceptance in the present moment.
- "A moment of self-compassion can change a day; a string of moments can change a life."

##### 4) Neutralize Negativity

- Others' coldness is often their discomfort, not about you.
- Negative thoughts assume perfect rationality about reality.
- Remember concentration is filtered; you never have the full picture.
- Move thoughts to an imaginary "writing on a wall" for inspection.
- Label thoughts and ask about their psychological source.
- Depersonalize: observe yourself as a human case study.
- Use a zoomed-out perspective ("look down from space").
- Imagine mental chatter as radio noise; adjust the volume.
- Recall past feelings and remember they passed.

##### 5) Rewrite Reality (Cognitive Reappraisal)

- Analyze anxiety and search for possible upside, even if imperfect.
- Write down alternative perspectives.
- For personal grievances, write feeling-filled letters and do not send them.
- Ask: "Will this matter in a month?"
- Change your environment (e.g., play calm music).

##### 6) Delve into Discomfort

- Practice uncomfortable situations with a trusted partner to build tolerance.
- Hold eye contact longer than socially expected.
- Notice and name sensations of discomfort.
- Explore each sensation directly.
- Build mild awkwardness and notice physical manifestations.
- Resist the urge to immediately relieve tension.
- With others:
  - Extend eye contact
  - Gently reduce personal distance
  - Enter elevator last while facing others
  - Start a conversation with a stranger

##### 7) Success Visualization

- Close eyes and recall a past triumph.
- Hear the sounds and feel smiles/admiration.
- Notice your feet on the ground and other physical sensations.
- Feel a rising warm glow.
- Visualize before important events.

##### 8) Build Gratitude

- Write down 3 things you are grateful for.
- Find three pleasant sights or meaningful nearby items.
- Describe life in positive terms from a third-person perspective.

##### 9) Generate Goodwill

- Remember people are fundamentally good.
- Respect people as they are.
- Give love freely.
- Imagine another person's past, then their present.
